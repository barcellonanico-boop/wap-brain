### Pricing reality 2026 (NEW — wasn't in v1.1 attempt)

This is PRIORITY content. The current live post has stale numbers. The April 27 attempt had no specific pricing.

**Airbnb (decent, not grandpa-pipes-in-drawer):**
- Centro Storico peak: **€120/night**
- Politeama/Libertà: **€90-100/night** (price drops as you move from Piazza Castelnuovo)
- Mondello summer: **€150+/night**
- Mondello winter: 30-40% less than summer (don't recommend stay)

**B&B with breakfast (Booking.com average):**
- Most areas summer: **€80-90/night**

**4-star hotels (couple, peak):**
- Hotel Politeama (4-star, renovated): **~€190/night**
- Range: €150-300/night

**5-star:**
- Grand Hotel et Des Palmes (Politeama): **~€600/night** — Nico's favorite
- Villa Igea (Rocco Forte, Acquasanta): **~€2,000/night** — "where Dua Lipa gets married" — top of the top

**The Airbnb economics insight (gold — must include):**

The real difference in Palermo isn't location, it's what's INSIDE the apartment. Cheap listings (€80) save by stripping basics: no oil, no salt, no soap, one crusted "WWII pan", three forks total, no bottle opener (go ask the neighbor). What you save in nightly rate you spend on groceries replacing missing essentials. Plus host economics: out of €100 paid on Airbnb, host sees ~€40 after Italy taxes + platform fees + cleaning. So suspiciously low prices = suspiciously empty apartments. Use this as the "apartment vs hotel" decision frame.

### Booking timing (NEW)

- **Best supply / lowest prices:** winter months (Nov-Feb, except Christmas)
- **Peak demand:** summer (Jun-Aug), Christmas/New Year, September-October (foreigners — weather calmer, beach still possible)
- **Specific event:** October has **La Via dei Tesori** (heritage event, super contested for accommodation)

### Italian local truth on neighborhood naming (LOCK)

**Hard rule:** Locals do NOT say "let's go to Kalsa" or "let's go to Albergheria" or "let's go to Monte di Pietà." For locals, it's all "Centro Storico" or "downtown" or just "il centro." Tourist guides break it into 4 names; locals merge them into one.

**Reference points locals use:** Quattro Canti = epicenter (use as ground zero for distance-from). Teatro Massimo / Piazza Verdi = where historic center "begins" (busy day and night, well-lit, safe).

**Strategy:** Mention the 4 sub-neighborhood names ONCE in Centro Storico section + once in FAQ (for SEO capture: people DO search "stay in Kalsa Palermo"). Then unify everything as Centro Storico for accommodation purposes. Frame: "Tourist guides break it into four neighborhoods. We don't. Pick a hotel anywhere inside Centro Storico, you're fine."

**Streets confirmed safe in Politeama/Libertà** (for women alone, families, first-timers):
Via Cavour, Via Villaermosa, Via Mariano Stabile, Via Principe di Belmonte, Via Ruggero Settimo, Via Wagner, Via Libertà, Via Gaetano Daita, Via La Lumia, Via Enrico Parisi, Via Messina, Via XII Gennaio, anything around Piazza Castelnuovo.
