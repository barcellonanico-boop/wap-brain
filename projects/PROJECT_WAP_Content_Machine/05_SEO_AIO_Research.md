## Executive Answer to the Core Question

Google organic SEO and Google AI Overviews are NOT optimized in different ways. They are optimized in the same way, with one layer added on top. The foundation is identical: strong topical authority, genuine first-hand experience, clear structure, solid technical hygiene. AI Overviews then add a second filter on top of that foundation — "can my answer be extracted as a self-contained chunk?"

Between 38% and 76% of AI Overview citations come from pages already ranking in Google's top 10 (sources disagree on the exact number). If you're not ranking organically, you're essentially invisible to AI Overviews. But ranking alone is not enough anymore: pages that rank #1 and write in dense, flowing prose without extractable chunks are getting skipped in favor of pages ranking #5 with cleaner structure.

The nuance: classic SEO (keyword + backlinks + content) still matters, but it's now table stakes, not the finish line. 2025-2026 updates (helpful content integrated into core, June 2025 "hidden gems" update, March 2024 refinements) have shifted reward toward small, personal, first-hand sites like WAP and AWAY from generic, keyword-stuffed content from big brands. Tripadvisor has publicly stated AI Overviews are breaking their traffic model. Travel queries saw 381% AI Overview growth between March 13-27, 2025. This is simultaneously WAP's biggest threat and biggest opportunity.

Bottom line for WAP: write for humans with genuine experience, structure every answer so a machine can lift it in one clean chunk, target specific long-tail Palermo queries that Lonely Planet ignores, and make sure Nico is visibly the author on every post.

## Section 1: THE RULES

### 1. Keyword and Topic Research Rules

1.1 Target long-tail, specific queries of 5+ words. AI Overviews appear on 99.9% of informational keywords, 46% long-tail. Broad head terms are answered directly in AI Overviews with zero clicks.

1.2 Use Ubersuggest + Google Search Console + Google autocomplete + "People Also Ask" as the full research stack. Do not pay for more tools.

1.3 Prioritize queries phrased as natural questions. 57.9% of AI Overview informational keywords are questions. Every post targets one primary question in the title.

1.4 Pick topics where Nico's first-hand experience is the differentiator. If Lonely Planet can write it without visiting, skip it.

1.5 Build topic clusters, not isolated posts. One pillar page links to 8-12 specific spoke posts. Entity-level ranking rewards this in 2026.

1.6 Do not chase head terms owned by massive brands. Pick long-tail variations they ignore. This is where the June 2025 "hidden gems" update hands WAP its window.

1.7 Ignore AI Overviews in low-trigger categories. Hotel/flight transactional queries show AI Overviews under 8%. Ranking-first is fine there.

### 2. Article Structure Rules

2.1 Answer the primary question in the first 100 words. 44% of AI Overview citations come from the first 30% of a page.

2.2 Include a direct-answer paragraph of 50-75 words right under the H1. This is the "TL;DR" block that AI Overviews extract. Must make sense with zero context.

2.3 Keep posts under 2,000 words as default, unless topic genuinely requires more (itineraries, comprehensive guides). Pages under 5,000 characters get 66% LLM extractability; over 20,000 characters only 12%. Padding hurts.

2.4 Every H2 answers one specific question phrased in natural language. Not "Best Time" — write "When is the best time to visit Palermo?"

2.5 Under every H2, first sentence is a direct self-contained answer. Context comes after.

2.6 Each paragraph is a self-contained unit. Paragraphs that need previous context will not get cited.

2.7 Short paragraphs, 2-4 sentences max. No walls of text. (Matches existing WAP rule.)

2.8 At least one bulleted or numbered list per post. Lists are the most citeable format in AI Overviews.

2.9 FAQ section at bottom, 4-6 questions. Each answer 40-75 words, stands alone. FAQPage schema required.

2.10 Each sentence is load-bearing: name entities, state relationship with clear verb, include specifics. Not "Palermo has great food" — "Palermo's historic center has four active street-food markets: Ballarò, Vucciria, Capo, Borgo Vecchio."

### 3. On-Page SEO Rules

3.1 Title tag includes primary question as a searcher types it, plus "Palermo" or specific location. 55-60 characters max.

3.2 Meta description answers the question in one sentence, 140-155 characters.

3.3 URL slug short, keyword-only. (Matches existing WAP pattern.)

3.4 Every post links internally to 3-5 related posts + 1-2 related hub pages.

3.5 Schema markup required on every post: Article, FAQPage, Author. Review schema if reviewing a place. RankMath, Yoast, or Schema Pro handles this.

3.6 One image every 300-400 words. Original photos. Descriptive alt text (not keyword-stuffed).

3.7 Visible publish date + "last updated" date. Freshness is now an active AI Overview ranking factor. 18+ months stale = deprioritized by Gemini.

3.8 Affiliate links clearly labeled. Google penalizes sites that funnel informational content into disguised affiliate links.

### 4. E-E-A-T and Authority Signals

4.1 Visible author byline with Nico's name linking to full author page. No exceptions.

4.2 Author page includes: photo, bio stating Palermo-born and resident, years of experience, YouTube videos, social profiles, press mentions.

4.3 Every post contains at least one first-person specific observation proving Nico was physically there. "I walked past the cathedral three mornings last week" — not "the cathedral is beautiful." Lily Ray's #1 differentiator for travel bloggers.

4.4 Original photos by Nico on every post.

4.5 Cite primary sources for facts, dates, numbers. Museum official site for hours. Official event organizer for dates.

4.6 Consistent author entity across WAP, YouTube, Instagram, guest posts. Same name, photo, bio wording. Entity consistency = Knowledge Graph resolution.

4.7 Link out to reputable external sources (UNESCO, official tourism boards) where relevant.

### 5. AI Overview-Specific Tactics

5.1 One 40-75 word "quotable chunk" early in every post that directly answers the target question. Self-contained.

5.2 Question-phrased H2s and H3s. Mirror exact searcher language.

5.3 Specific numbers, dates, prices, named entities in every answer block. "Palermo Cathedral costs €15 and is open Monday-Saturday 9am-6pm" beats "tickets are reasonably priced."

5.4 FAQ block with FAQPage schema on every post. Single most-cited format in AI Overviews.

5.5 Comparison content as tables. "Palermo vs Catania for first-time visitors" as a table, not prose. Tables get lifted directly.

5.6 One "definitive list" post per topic cluster. Lists rank disproportionately in AI Overviews.

5.7 Target query fan-out: cover immediate follow-up questions in the same post. "Is Palermo safe" post covers "safe at night," "safe for solo women," "areas to avoid" all in one place.

5.8 Update top-performing posts every 6 months. Dated note at top: "Updated April 2026 with new ticket prices."

5.9 Clean crawlable HTML. No heavy JavaScript gating. 46% of ChatGPT bot visits use reading mode.

### 6. What NOT to Do — Dead or Penalized in 2026

6.1 Do NOT chase keyword density. Dead.

6.2 Do NOT publish generic "10 things to do in Palermo" content that could be written without visiting. Hit by Helpful Content classifier.

6.3 Do NOT publish long-for-long's-sake posts. Over 5,000 chars dilutes extractability. Over 20,000 chars actively hurts.

6.4 Do NOT use AI to generate drafts without substantial human rewrite. Google doesn't penalize AI content per se (Ahrefs 600k-page study) but penalizes scaled generic output.

6.5 Do NOT bury the answer under intro. "In this post we'll explore..." wastes the highest-value real estate.

6.6 Do NOT publish thin content or doorway pages. Group similar variations on one page.

6.7 Do NOT disguise affiliate links as informational recommendations without disclosure.

6.8 Do NOT let content sit untouched for 2+ years.

6.9 Do NOT let unmoderated blog comments drag down site E-E-A-T.

6.10 Do NOT rely on single primary keyword per post. Cover the topic semantically, don't repeat phrases.

6.11 Do NOT add nosnippet/noindex to "protect" from AI Overviews. Removes you from all snippets AND traditional SERP features.

6.12 Do NOT publish solely for AI search. Organic clicks still matter. Both matter.
