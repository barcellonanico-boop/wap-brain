## Escalation 1 — WAP_05 is broken as a teaching doc. Highest priority.

WAP_05 is a comprehensive rulebook. It has 21 named voice moves, Mode A vs Mode B framework, banned-in-body-copy words, paragraph rules, signature pattern descriptions, the Italian-American comedian persona definition, the Sebastian Maniscalco style anchor.

What it's missing: annotated voice samples from Nico's actual published work.

I read WAP_05 three times this session. I executed every rule on the page. The Pass 2 v1 output read like a memo with extra periods. The Pass 2 v2 output applied italics on top of v1 prose and called it voice install. Both failed Nico's review on the same complaint: "the rhythm isn't there."

The diagnosis: reading "short sentences, Maniscalco rhythm, signature moves" and writing in the actual voice are two different skills. The doc tells you the rules. It doesn't show you the feel. The rules alone don't compose. You need worked examples to see how the moves combine into rhythm.

### The fix

Create **WAP_05b — Voice in Action.**

- Take Nico's Palermo Tourist Information article (already on the site, Nico-approved gold standard).
- Paste the entire article in the doc.
- Annotate every paragraph: which signature moves it uses, why each one lands, what the rhythm pattern is.
- Mark where italics-for-stress lands vs bold-for-topics.
- Mark every "you sit down, you order, you blink" second-person immersion pattern.
- Mark every disarming opener ("Look, I'll level with you," "I know, I know").
- Mark every mocking imagined dialogue ("It was dirty!" "The traffic was awful!").
- Mark every hyperbolic specific ("Your internal organs will start to slow-cook").
- Mark every Italian/Sicilian color drop with translation pattern.

The copywriter then has WAP_05 for the rules and WAP_05b for the feel. Both required reading every session.

**Without WAP_05b, every voice pass run by any copywriter (including me) will under-deliver. We proved that twice today.**

## Escalation 2 — SOP_01 word-count target conflicts with voice density.

Current Pass 2 target: 3,000-3,500 words.

This target assumes voice install tightens word count. It doesn't. Real Maniscalco rhythm needs setup-pause-payoff structure. Reader self-select forks. Mocking imagined dialogue. Hyperbolic specifics. All of these cost words.

Pass 2 v1 of Favignana hit 3,455 words and read like a memo. The locked v3 with proper voice density landed at ~3,755 (+8.7% over v1). That's voice install in destination-guide content. Smaller posts will scale similarly.

### The fix

Replace the single 3,000-3,500 target with content-type-specific bands in SOP_01 Step 8:

- **Quick-answer posts** (FAQ-style, <1,500 word original): tighten by 10-15% during voice install
- **Destination guides** (3,000-5,000 word original): hold within ±10% of Pass 1 v2.1 word count; voice install adds words and that's expected
- **Long-form pillars** (5,000+ word original): voice install can run +15-20%

Add the rule explicitly: **word count is downstream of voice. Trim padding mercilessly. Never trim comedic builds.**

## Escalation 4 — New rule: Credibility lives in author intro, not in every H2.

Discovered this session.

When I opened H2 1 with "I'm Nico, born in Palermo, been coming here since X," that read as throat-clearing. The author intro upstream had already done that job. By the time the reader hits H2 1, they know who I am.

Mid-article credibility re-introductions dilute the H2's actual job: directly engaging the question in its own heading.

### The fix

Add to WAP_06 (or SOP_01 Step 8 voice install rules, wherever the PM thinks it lives best):

> **Credibility rule.** Each H2 should open by directly engaging the question in its own heading, not by re-establishing who's writing. First-person experience can and should appear inside H2s, but as observations woven into the answer ("a typical day for me," "I bike between coves," "the one time I brought a Nordic friend over"), not as a fresh self-introduction. The author intro at the top of the article does the credibility job once. H2s deliver insight.

## Escalation 5 — New rule: Author intro structure (5 jobs).

Discovered earlier in the project but worth re-confirming.

The old WAP_06 author intro rule was "name + credibility + sales hook" in 80 words. That's not enough.

The new rule, validated by Nico across multiple iterations:

> **Author intro 5 jobs.** Name + credibility + the problem (what tourist blogs get wrong) + a concise bullet overview of the article + USP. ~120-150 words. Bullet list mandatory.

Two more sub-rules from this session:

**5a. Open with positive, not negatives.** The intro should make the reader want to keep reading AND want to consider the destination. Negatives in the intro break both. The brutally-honest positioning is established by promising hard truths, not by listing them upfront.

**5b. Connect the credibility facts as one flowing thought, not three disconnected sentences.** "Born in Palermo. Grandma swimming in 1962. Going since I was teething." reads as three flat facts. "Born in Palermo, going every summer since I was teething, and my grandmother was already swimming there in 1962" reads as one connected idea ending on a punchline.

## Escalation 6 — New rule: Hidden gem reframing for tourist-secret content.

Discovered this session, applies to any post that addresses a "hidden" or "secret" or "off the beaten path" cliche.

**The wrong frame:** "It's not hidden. It's the most packed island in Sicily by August."

**The right frame:** "It's hidden from foreigners. From locals? It's been packed since the eighties. The 'secret' is only news to tourists."

The first frame makes the destination sound less appealing. The second frame both kills the cliche AND establishes insider/outsider, which is the actual cultural truth and what the audience needs to hear.

### The fix

Add to WAP_05 (voice rules) or WAP_06 (content format) under cliche-killer patterns:

> **Tourist-secret reframing.** When killing a "hidden gem" / "off the beaten path" / "locals love it" cliche, frame the secret as insider-vs-outsider, not as "this is overcrowded." The destination should still sound desirable. The tourist should feel called out for not knowing what locals already know.

## Escalation 7 — New brain doc needed: WAP_14_TARGET_AUDIENCE.md.

Flagged earlier but escalating again.

We don't have a permanent reference doc that defines who WAP's audience is, what they know, and what they don't know. This gap produced the "vs Ustica" mistake at the top of Pass 1 v1 (I assumed Americans know what Ustica is — they don't). It also produces other audience-blindness errors.

### The fix

Create **WAP_14_TARGET_AUDIENCE.md.** Defines:

- **Who they are** (American/UK/Australian/Canadian travelers planning a Sicily trip; income tier; age band; trip-planning style)
- **What they DO know** about Sicily, Italy, Mediterranean travel, Italian food
- **What they DON'T know** that we have to explain in plain English (e.g., minor Italian island names; Italian regional differences; Italian holidays; Italian meal timing)
- **What jokes/references work for them** (American interstate system, New York hotel prices, Mykonos as a partying-island anchor, "blue Gatorade" as a color reference)
- **What jokes/references don't work** (Italian regional in-jokes that need translation; Sicilian dialect references that don't earn their place; obscure European references)

Copywriter consults every session. Architect consults during 04_Structural_Audit when planning Reader Intent.

## Escalation 8 — The voice formula (11 moves) for SOP/Brain doc inclusion.

This is the operational rule for every Pass 2 going forward. Locked this session through three iterations with Nico. Lock it in WAP_05 wherever the PM thinks it lives best.

### The Nico Voice Formula (Mode A destination guides)

**Critical preamble:**
The formula is reusable structural moves, NOT recycled phrases. Each new post reaches for fresh hyperbolic specifics, fresh mocking imagined dialogue, fresh disarming openers. The locked Favignana article is the reference for rhythm and density, not for content. Death-by-template is the failure mode this preamble exists to prevent.

**The 11 moves:**

1. **Disarming opener** ("Look," "Listen," "I'll level with you," "I know, I know")
2. **Reader self-select fork** within first 30 seconds where it fits ("Are you the kind of person who...? Stop reading. Book Mykonos.") — pulls reader in, makes them self-categorize
3. **First-person experience** specific to the section, woven in as observations not as a fresh self-introduction ("a typical day for me," "I once brought a Nordic friend over")
4. **One specific, vivid joke per section beat.** Hyperbolic specifics, not generic descriptors. "Tuna so fresh the fish was making plans this morning," NOT "great fresh fish." "Faster than the last cannolo at a baptism," NOT "they sell out fast."
5. **Italics for vocal stress, NOT bold.** Bold marks topics for skimmers. Italics marks voice emphasis for readers. Conflating them flattens the voice.
6. **Mocking imagined dialogue** at least once per H2 if it lands. ("wait, what bottle are we on?" / "It was dirty!" / "My friend, you work three months a year and you still close two hours mid-afternoon?")
7. **Italian/Sicilian color** where it earns its place with translation in parens for unfamiliar terms. Never pattern-matched filler. (*aperitivo, pane cunzato, niente, mattanza, riposino*)
8. **Setup-pause-payoff rhythm.** Long setup, beat, short slap. NOT period-period-period staccato fragments.
9. **Second-person immersion** for sensory beats ("you sit down, you order one drink, you blink, it's nine PM") to put the reader IN the scene
10. **Max 3 sentences / 45 words per paragraph.** No exceptions. Walls of text are the cardinal sin.
11. **Section closers earn a slap.** ("That's Favignana." "Go now." "We're not offended." "Treat it wrong and it'll humble you.")
