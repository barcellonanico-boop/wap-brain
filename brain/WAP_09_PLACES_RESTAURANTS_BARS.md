If you want a piazzetta vibe, market noise, no-frills atmosphere, and pasta that actually tastes like someone's nonna made it — this is your spot.

### R002 — Trattoria Pisci e Trema (officially Trattoria da Pino)

- **Category:** Trattoria
- **Area:** Borgo Vecchio (between Historic Center and Politeama/Libertà)
- **Address:** Via dello Spezio, 6, Palermo
- **Google Maps:** https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Trattoria+da+Pino+Via+dello+Spezio+Palermo
- **TripAdvisor:** [VERIFY TA URL]
- **Tags:** borgo-vecchio, sicilian-traditional, lunch-only, no-frills, hidden-gem, casual, locals-only, family
- **Price:** €€
- **Must-try:** Ask Giuseppe (the owner) for the day's specials — there is no printed menu
- **Best for:** Lunch only. Not open for dinner.
- **Recommended by:** [VERIFY]
- **Last visited:** 2025 (Nico)
- **Status:** Active

**Nico's Take:**

The real name is Trattoria da Pino. But nobody calls it that. Everyone calls it "Pisci e Trema" — it's a Sicilian expression, and there's a story behind it.

Back in the day, Pino's father was taking a leak in the bathroom when the Guardia di Finanza (Italian tax police) showed up for a surprise inspection. The waiter runs in: "Signore, la Finanza è qua!" The guy starts trembling so hard he's pissing and shaking at the same time. Pisci e trema. Pees and trembles. The name stuck.

Why? Because back then, a receipt from this place? You'd have better luck finding a unicorn. The Guardia showing up was code red.

The place itself is classic Borgo Vecchio. No menu. Home cooking, poor-people's cooking — in the best possible sense. Cheap. Ask Giuseppe what's good today, eat whatever he brings, don't overthink it.

Lunch only. Don't show up at dinner expecting it to be open. The reservation system is a mess, service is simple, atmosphere is casual. Capisci?
