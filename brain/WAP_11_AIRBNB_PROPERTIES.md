## Electrical System — Critical Known Quirk

**The apartment trips the breaker if too many high-draw appliances run simultaneously.** This is the #1 preventable guest issue and needs to be communicated clearly upfront.

### Appliances that will blow the circuit if combined

- Oven + hair dryer + AC running at the same time
- Washing machine + oven + AC
- Any combination of 3 heavy appliances = almost guaranteed trip

### Rule for guests

Run ONE high-draw appliance at a time. Wait for it to finish before starting another. Small stuff (lights, phone chargers, TV, coffee machine) doesn't count.

### If the power goes out — 2-step troubleshooting

**Step 1 — Inside the apartment:**
- Electrical panel is located between the main apartment door and the window, on the LEFT side of the main door.
- All levers on the panel must be UP. If any are DOWN, flip them back UP.
- If power returns: done.

**Step 2 — If Step 1 doesn't fix it, go down to the ground floor:**
- Stand inside the building entrance, face the main building door from the inside.
- On the RIGHT there is a white door. Open it.
- The electricity meter is inside. The relevant one is BOTTOM RIGHT.
- The lever on it must be UP. If DOWN, flip it UP.
- Power should return.

### Prevention script for guest messages

If a guest mentions they're planning to do laundry and cook a big meal, proactively remind them: one heavy appliance at a time.

## Shower Drain — Known Quirk

- The shower has a gray metal drain cover on the floor (a small flat grate).
- It catches hair, soap residue, and debris to keep the pipe clear.
- On long stays, this fills up and water stops draining properly.
- **Fix:** Gently lift the metal cover (a butter knife works to wedge it up), pull out the debris (hair etc.), rinse it off, put it back.
- **Cleaning crew handles this between stays** — new guests start with a clean drain.
- **Long-stay guests need to do it themselves at least once** during their stay, otherwise the shower floods.
- **If ignored:** shower water backs up, floods the bathroom floor, soaks the porous stone. Bad outcome.

- **Free street parking (not guaranteed, first come first serve):**
  - Piazza Sant'Eligio (closest to the apartment)
  - Via Giovanni Meli
  - Via Castello
  - Surrounding streets in the area
- **Illegal parking valets ("parcheggiatori abusivi"):** Common in Palermo. When guests arrive to park, someone often approaches and "helps" them park, then asks for money.
  - Nico's advice to guests: give €1 max if you want to avoid friction. Or pretend you don't speak Italian and walk away. Don't engage in negotiation.
