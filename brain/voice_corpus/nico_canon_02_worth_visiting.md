## Is San Vito Worth It?

Yes. With a big but. Stay with me so you don't screw this up.

San Vito Lo Capo is sea, food, nap, repeat.

That's the whole operating system.

The most exciting thing to do here is look at your feet in perfect detail even when the water is past your crotch.

This is not the place for "uzz uzz uzz" beach clubs, gin and tonics at 10 AM, and matching with Mario on Tinder.

It's also not the place for church tours where you pretend to know the difference between Baroque and Neoclassical while secretly wanting a cannolo in your face.

For that, go to Palermo or Catania.

San Vito is for flip-flops, swimsuits, and one moral dilemma:

Gelato now, or gelato after the second swim? If that's your trip, good. You found it.

If you need "something to happen" every hour, change destination before your nervous system files a complaint.

But here's the catch.

August is not the best month to come.

Italians go on vacation in August. All of them. The country stops. Don't go to jail. Don't die. Nobody is available.

Translated?

San Vito gets assaulted by half the country.

Those dreamy photos you saw?

Taken at 6 AM in June with a fisherman and a depressed seagull.

Show up at noon on August 15th and your patch of sand will be wedged between a Naples family of seven, three unused kayaks, and Riccardo selling coconut from a cooler.

Can you still have a beautiful August trip?

Yes. Just expect more people, higher prices, and less Instagram magic.

If you have flexibility, come in late May, June, September, or the first ten days of October.

That's when locals go. That's when the water does the Photoshop thing on its own.

And if August is your only window?

Drive twenty minutes south to Castellammare del Golfo instead.

More space. Beautiful water. Charming village. Far fewer human limbs per square meter.

> **Local's Take.** In August, I do Castellammare. I leave at 9:00 AM. Around 9:50, I stop at La Sorgente café for a cassatella, a ricotta pastry that exists mainly to remind you life isn't completely terrible. By 10:10, I'm on the beach at Lido Pirata or Peter Pan. At 5:00 PM, I leave. By 6:00 PM, I'm back home in Palermo, salty, fed, and not psychologically destroyed by August in San Vito.
