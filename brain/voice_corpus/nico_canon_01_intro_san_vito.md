Allora. You're coming to Sicily.

You watched John and Mary on YouTube screaming, "You absolutely have to see San Vito Lo Capo, the Caribbean of Italy!"

They showed the white beach, clear water, and little fish saying ciao over that lobotomy-inducing travel-vlog ukulele.

You nudged your partner: "John says we gotta go." Now you're here looking for reality.

I hear you. No ukulele here. No fake drone-shot wisdom.

I'll tell you what I'd tell my cousin if she landed in Palermo tomorrow.

And what John desperately needs to hear before he calls another beach "hidden gem" with 9,000 people on it.

If we haven't met, I'm Nico Barcellona. Born, raised, and sustained by Palermo cannoli.

I've been going to San Vito for 39 years. Basically since my mom's pregnancy test gave my dad chest pain.

This is the advice I give when someone is actually listening. Not the version designed to sell you a $12 snorkel set on Amazon.

**What you'll learn**

- The months with postcard water, and the exact month every local avoids like the plague.
- Stay in San Vito or day-trip from Palermo? (Spoiler: most people get this dead wrong).
- Real 2026 prices for a beach chair, a decent bed, a plate of couscous, and the parking ticket they will slap on your windshield the second you blink.
