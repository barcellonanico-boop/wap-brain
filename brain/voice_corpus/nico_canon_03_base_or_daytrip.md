## Stay in San Vito or Day-Trip From Palermo?

Allora, it's time to pull out the word everybody hates:

Depends.

Answer one simple question.

Is swimming, eating, napping, and slowly becoming a sun-dried tomato the main purpose of your trip?

If the answer is a solid yes, base yourself in San Vito for 2 or 3 days.

Pick a hotel inside the walkable village. Drop the car keys in a drawer. Tell your mother-in-law the phone signal is bad.

Beach in the morning. Lunch at the lido or in a trattoria. A drool-inducing nap under your umbrella.

Dinner. Gelato. Bed. Repeat. Beautiful.

Do that for two or three days and you'll leave town quietly wondering if you should sell your apartment and become the guy who rents beach umbrellas.

But I wouldn't base there for more than 5 days.

San Vito is small. Charming, yes. But small.

Once you've done the beach, the seafront walk, and the couscous, you've basically done San Vito.

Three days in, you'll start photographing your gelato just to feel alive.

If your answer is, "Yes, Nico, beach is nice, but I also want to see stuff and do stuff," then don't base in San Vito.

You can use it to visit Erice, Monte Cofano, Trapani, or Segesta, sure.

But as a base for a richer Sicily trip? Meh.

My move if you're flying into Palermo: Stay 3 or 4 nights in Palermo. Do one full day in San Vito.

Drive up at 8 AM, beach all day, have dinner there or on the way back, then continue with the rest of your route.

You get the beach. You don't waste your evenings.

> **Local's Take:** Stay in San Vito if your ultimate goal is moving your ass from a beach chair to the water and back. Stay in Palermo if sitting on a towel for more than an hour makes you want to commit hara-kiri.
