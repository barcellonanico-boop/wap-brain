Last updated: May 5, 2026
Source: 9-dataset audience analysis (08_Audience_Analysis_May5.md) + Ubersuggest top 30 organic pages

---

## Purpose

This is the canonical persona library. The agent uses these schede in Fase 2 of the v2.3 workflow (Persona Match) to decide WHO is reading the article being written, then adapts content depth, tone callibration, knowledge assumptions, CTA placement, and affiliate selection to that persona.

3 personas only. Not 5, not 10. The data shows 3 distinct clusters that explain ~95% of the audience and revenue. Adding more personas dilutes the signal.

Every article targets ONE primary persona + optionally ONE secondary. Never 3-at-once.

## Persona A — "The Anglo Couple Planner"

### Identity
35-55 years old. Female-led couple planning. Anglo-saxon (USA/UK/AU/CA). College-educated, dual-income household, leisure international traveler. The dominant audience and dominant revenue source.

### Demographics (from data)
- **Age:** 25-34 (24% site) + 45-54 (22% site) — two clusters, both planners
- **Gender:** Female 60% / Male 40% on site (woman is the planner)
- **Geography:** UK 20% + USA 20% + Australia 4% + Canada 4% = ~50% of site traffic
- **Device:** Mobile 67% reading, desktop 50% checkout (reads on iPhone, books from laptop)
- **Income:** mid-to-upper. €100-300/night hotel range (Booking €24/booking avg commission)

### Psychographics
- **Values:** authenticity, safety, efficiency. Doesn't want a "tourist trap" experience.
- **Fears:** scams (Airbnb fake listings, taxi rip-off, ZTL fines, pickpockets), bad food, wasted vacation days
- **Decision style:** thorough researcher. Reads 5-10 articles before booking. 3-4 month planning horizon.
- **Self-image:** "smart traveler, not a basic tourist." Wants to feel like she's getting insider info.

### Search behavior
- Top keywords: "where to stay Palermo", "is Palermo safe", "Palermo street food", "best beaches Palermo", "Mondello Palermo", "Palermo nightlife"
- Uses Google primarily, English language
- Reads on phone during commute / lunch break
- Books from laptop in evening

### Buying behavior
- **Booking.com hotels:** primary revenue contribution. Mid-range (€80-200/night) to occasional luxury (€600+).
- **GetYourGuide tours:** walking tours (Street Food €854 top), bar crawl, wine tasting, Mafia Walking, San Vito Boat
- **Sicilian Way (Stripe):** ~50% of buyers. Pays full $47 or downsell $31 2-3 months pre-trip.
- **Peak booking:** April-July (planning) → June-September (staying). Sep peak in commission.
- **Decision speed:** medium. Reads multiple articles, compares, then commits.

### Voice match (what works for her)
- **Maniscalco-flavored authenticity:** "I'm 100% Sicilian, I'll tell you what's real"
- **Brutal honesty about tourist traps:** validates her fear of scams
- **Specific numbers:** "€160 ZTL fine," "€80 ripoff Airbnb," "Bus 806 to Mondello 30-40 min"
- **Insider framing:** "where I'd send my sister," "what locals actually do"
- **Anti-vlogger positioning:** "I'm not the umpteenth John from Manchester"

### Knowledge gaps (must explain)
- ZTL (Limited Traffic Zone) — concept doesn't exist in USA/UK
- Bidet — yes, must explain it's for ass-cleaning daily, not champagne cooler
- Italian neighborhood granularity (Vucciria/Kalsa/Capo) — confusing, just say Centro Storico
- AMAT (public transport) — local acronyms
- Italian cultural quirks (8 PM dinner, Sunday closures, August shutdown)

### Channels
- **Site (Google search):** dominant. ~75% of touchpoints.
- **YouTube:** rare. She's not on YouTube for planning.
- **Meta Ads:** not in this audience.

### Revenue contribution
- **~60-70% of total revenue** (€17,000-€20,000/year)
- Highest volume, medium per-customer LTV
- One trip, then probably gone (no newsletter, no return tracking)

### Top articles for this persona (validated by Ubersuggest)

## Persona B — "The Italo-American Heritage Seeker (50+)"

### Identity
45-65+ years old. Second or third generation Sicilian-American (or Sicilian-Australian). Returning to Sicily for emotional connection with family roots, not just tourism. High lifetime value because of long stays + premium tour purchases.

### Demographics (from data)
- **Age:** 45-54 (51% YouTube 90-day) + 65+ (30% YouTube 90-day) — older skew
- **Gender:** Male 67% / Female 33% on YouTube. Mixed couple consuming content together on TV.
- **Geography:** USA dominant (Florida, California, New York specific clusters). Canada secondary. Pockets in Australia and UK from Sicilian diaspora.
- **Device:** YouTube on Smart TV 41% (most of any YouTube device). Site reading on iPad/Desktop.
- **Income:** higher disposable. Long stays 20-30 days, premium hotels (Villa Igiea €2,000/night), private tours (Corleone Mafia €166 commission per booking).

### Psychographics
- **Values:** identity, heritage, return-to-roots, family stories. Wants to feel "I belong here."
- **Fears:** being treated like a generic tourist, missing the "real" Sicily, getting scammed because they're foreign-but-Italian-named
- **Decision style:** emotional + thorough. Spends months consuming content (YouTube + site). Books high-ticket items.
- **Self-image:** "I'm coming home, not visiting." Often visiting specific ancestral towns (Villafrati, San Cataldo, Sferracavallo, Corleone).

### Search behavior
- Top keywords: "Sicilian Mafia history," "Sicilian people," "Corleone Sicily," "[ancestral village name] Sicily," "Sicilian roots"
- YouTube discovery dominant (47% of touchpoints from YouTube)
- Watches videos on TV in evening with spouse (passive intent → research mode)
- Multi-month consideration period
- Often plans multi-week trips with regional itinerary (Palermo + Cefalù + ancestral village + maybe Catania)

### Buying behavior
- **Booking.com hotels:** longer stays = more commission per trip (20-30 nights). Premium leaning.
- **GetYourGuide:** premium niche tours. Vintage Fiat 500 (€536 commission per booking, only 16 sales but high-value), Corleone Private Mafia Tour (€166 commission per booking, 4 sales)
- **Sicilian Way (Stripe):** ~25-30% of buyers. Italian heritage cognomi visible in customer list (Asaro, Tellini, Pittari, Conti, Ferrara).
- **Peak booking:** Sep-Oct planning for spring/Christmas trips. Long lead time.
- **Decision speed:** slow but sticky. High loyalty once converted.

### Voice match (what works for him/her)
- **"I'm 100% Sicilian, born in Palermo, 39 years here, plus my mom's nine months of pregnancy"** — identity recognition. Critical opener.
- **Italian sprinkled naturally:** "Mamma mia," "u cannolu" — signals authenticity
- **Brutal honesty about Sicily's flaws + protection:** "I'll be your local brother who tells you the truth"
- **Family/community framing:** "this is what nonna's grandmother used to make"
- **Anti-American-Italian sentiment when warranted:** mocking the "Olive Garden Italian" stereotypes they grew up around
- **Edutainment:** they watch even when not planning. Humor + practical info both work.

### Knowledge gaps (must explain)
- Italian regional differences (Sicily ≠ Campania ≠ Tuscany — they may know "Italian" but not the regional layer)
- Modern Sicily vs the Sicily their grandparents left (it's not 1950 anymore)
- How to find ancestral records / civil registries
- Practical logistics for visiting small inland towns (rental car needed, no public transport)

### Channels
- **YouTube:** dominant for discovery. Heavy TV consumption.
- **Site:** secondary, used for deep research after YouTube hooks them.
- **Meta Ads:** marginal but present (65+ Women dominant in Cheat Sheet ads).
- **Future newsletter (project):** this is the persona that would justify a newsletter. Continuity content — Sicilian recipes, family heritage stories, monthly culture deep-dives — would retain them year-round.

### Revenue contribution
- **~25-35% of total revenue** (€7,000-€10,000/year estimated)
- Lower volume than Persona A but higher per-customer value
- Sticky — would buy multiple things over time if a continuity product existed

### Top articles for this persona (validated by Ubersuggest)
- /the-history-of-sicilian-mafia/ (494 traffic, 193 backlinks — high-authority)
- /sicilian-people/ (178 traffic, 255 backlinks — pure brand)
- /news/top-things-see-corleone/ (Mafia ancestry tourism)
- /history-of-palermo/
- /news/sicilian-street-food-specialities/ (overlap with A — they care about real food)
- Future opportunity: ancestry guide, "small towns of inland Sicily," "tracing Sicilian roots"

## Persona C — "The Returning Italy Traveler"

### Identity
30-50 years old. Sophisticated traveler who has already done Tuscany, Rome, Amalfi, maybe Lake Como. Now seeking the South of Italy because the North feels overdone. Lower volume but high engagement. Values anti-tourist positioning.

### Demographics (from data)
- **Age:** 35-44 + 45-54 mostly. Wider distribution.
- **Gender:** balanced
- **Geography:** Mix UK + USA + Northern Europe (Germany 5%, Netherlands 3%, France 2%, Sweden, Belgium)
- **Device:** Mix mobile/desktop, balanced
- **Income:** mid-to-upper. Compares prices, willing to spend for quality but not blindly luxury.

### Psychographics
- **Values:** depth, authenticity, off-the-beaten-path. Hates feeling like a basic tourist.
- **Fears:** "having a Tuscany experience" — being herded into the same Instagram spots as everyone else
- **Decision style:** comparison-heavy researcher. Reads competitor content, cross-references. Slower converter.
- **Self-image:** "experienced traveler who knows better than the average tourist."

### Search behavior
- Top keywords: "off the beaten path Sicily," "best places Sicily not touristy," "Aeolian Islands," "real Sicily," "[smaller town] guide"
- Reads multiple sources. Will leave if content feels generic.
- Books later in planning cycle (compare more options)

### Buying behavior
- **Booking.com:** mid-range. Boutique stays preferred over chains.
- **GetYourGuide:** less likely to book group tours (sees them as "tourist"). Goes for private/unusual tours.
- **Sicilian Way:** ~10-15% of buyers. Slowest converter.
- **Decision speed:** slow. Read 8-15 articles before deciding.

### Voice match (what works for them)
- **Anti-tourist-vlogger positioning:** "I'm not the umpteenth John from Manchester" — signals you're not the basic blogger they've been ignoring
- **Cultural depth + brutality:** willingness to call out generic guides + tourist traps
- **Specificity:** prefers "Bus 806 takes 30-40 min from Politeama to Mondello, costs €1.50" over "easy public transport"
- **Skepticism welcomed:** "yeah, the guides told you Vucciria. Here's what they didn't tell you."

### Knowledge gaps (must explain)
- Even if traveled, may not know Sicily-specific things (ZTL, AMAT, Sicilian dialect quirks)
- Regional differences within Sicily (Western vs Eastern Sicilian culture)
- Smaller Aeolian/Egadi islands

### Channels
- **Site:** dominant. Heavy Google research.
- **YouTube:** occasional. Not their primary channel.

### Revenue contribution
- **~5-10% of total revenue** (€1,400-€2,800/year estimated)
- Low volume, low-medium LTV
- High engagement (reads many articles) but low conversion rate

### Top articles for this persona (validated by Ubersuggest)

- /news/guide-to-catania-for-first-time-visitors/ (62 traffic but good niche)

## How Fase 2 of the v2.3 Workflow Uses This

Each new article enters Fase 2 (Persona Match). The agent:

1. Reads the article topic (from Fase 1 Tech Report).
2. Reads the GSC keyword data for the article.
3. Maps the keyword pattern + topic to ONE primary persona (and optionally one secondary).
4. Outputs `02_Persona.md` with:
   - Primary persona ID (A, B, or C)
   - Secondary persona ID if relevant (or "none")
   - Justification (why this persona for this article)
   - Specific persona attributes that matter most for THIS article
   - Knowledge gaps to address
   - Voice calibration notes

5. Subsequent fasi (4-7) read 02_Persona.md and adapt accordingly.

## Anti-Patterns (what NOT to do)

- **Don't try to write for all 3 personas in one article.** Pick one primary, max one secondary. Generic "for everyone" content converts no one.
- **Don't assume Persona B knows Italian.** They may have Sicilian heritage but speak only English.
- **Don't write for Persona C in entry-level articles.** They want depth. Entry-level content (Palermo Safety 101, ZTL basics) is for Persona A.
- **Don't write voice that flatters the reader.** All 3 personas dislike sycophancy. Brutal honesty + humor = Nico's USP.
- **Don't add Persona D-YouTube as a 4th persona for site articles.** YouTube is a separate channel. Site articles target A/B/C only.
