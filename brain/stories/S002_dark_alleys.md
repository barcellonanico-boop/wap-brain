## Story Body

Palermo's historic center is a labyrinth of tiny alleys and lanes. Some with lighting dimmer than my Uncle Luigi's garage. Others pitch black like the mayor forgot to pay the electric bill.

For us locals, just regular old streets. We walk down these streets like we own the place, maybe humming a little Sinatra under our breath.

But for tourists? These streets look straight outta The Godfather! At the slightest noise, you're thinking, "This is it! Don Corleone's henchmen are gonna make me sleep with the fishes tonight!"

Here's some advice so your imagination doesn't turn your trip into a thriller movie. Stay near the main streets. More light, more people, and you ain't gotta crap yourself in fear every time you head back to the hotel.
